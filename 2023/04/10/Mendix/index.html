微流

微流简介

微流可以为您的应用程序添加逻辑。 逻辑用于使您的应用程序更智能、自动化并使其可维护。

简单的按钮仅仅作为如：CreateObject、Show A Page 等默认行为，对于复杂的业务可以使用微流进行处理。

微流的作用：

  • 扩展或改变默认行为
  • 处理特殊的业务流程
  • 集成外部系统、数据库、Web 服务

原生移动应用简介

引言

在开始开发之前，Robin需要决定将哪种移动技术用于安全屋应用程序。有三个选项需要考虑：

  1. 响应式 Web 应用在移动设备的 Web 浏览器中运行。现代 Web 浏览器提供了使用户界面适应移动屏幕的能力，并可以访问许多设备功能，例如拍照或访问用户的位置。如果应用不需要脱机运行，并且 Web 浏览器提供的功能足够，则响应式 Web 应用是一个不错的选择。
  2. 渐进式 Web 应用 （PWA） 也在 Web 浏览器中运行，但会向响应式 Web 应用添加其他功能，例如存储脱机数据并让用户将其添加为主屏幕的快捷方式。如果脱机运行很重要，但不需要与操作系统进行更深入的集成，则渐进式 Web 应用是一个不错的选择。请注意此处记录的 西门子低代码 PWA 的限制。
  3. 本机移动应用程序直接在操作系统上运行。它通常通过“商店”（Apple AppStore或Google PlayStore）安装，并运行可以访问设备所有功能的本机代码，包括本机用户界面元素。如果出色的用户体验很重要或需要与移动设备进行高级集成，则本机应用程序是一个不错的选择。

Robin对The Safe House应用程序有两个重要要求：首先，它应该提供出色的用户体验，因为他的员工很年轻，并且习惯于Instagram和Uber等提供这种体验的应用程序。其次，该应用程序应该离线运行，因为他管理的许多公寓都没有Wifi，只有不良的蜂窝连接。出于这些原因，他决定构建一个原生移动应用程序。但这对应用程序的开发意味着什么？

Team Values

Naturally, each team needs to find its own drive, but in the essence, they should focus on the following 5 values:

  • Focus – As teams begin to multitask, the quality of their work starts to drop, and the time to completion increases. Scrum encourages team members to take on only a few things, thus giving each item the attention it deserves. By breaking the app into smaller Sprints and limiting the flow of work into those Sprints, Scrum helps to focus on each item more tightly.
  • Courage – Because a small, cross-functional team can pool their skills, and because Scrum encourages close collaboration, the team has the courage to take on greater challenges than they would as individuals.
  • Openness – This is one of the most important values of the Scrum framework. Scrum teams can achieve openness through regular reviews with stakeholders and regular planning and communication with their peers.
  • Commitment – Scrum teams commit to always delivering the highest quality of work possible, always being transparent about the true status of each item with stakeholders. Also, Scrum teams commit to a specific amount of work being done in each Sprint. This commitment builds trust with the stakeholders.
  • Respect – Respect in a Scrum team is important to enabling openness in communication, so each team member feels safe to share their impediments, and the team can help each other with these impediments.

As you engage in a real project, you’ll build people knowledge, contribute to building a team spirit, deliver successful projects, making customers successful, but don’t forget to have fun and enjoy the work you do!

团队价值观

当然，每个团队都需要找到自己的动力，但本质上，他们应该关注以下5个价值观:

专注——当团队开始多任务时，他们的工作质量开始下降，完成工作的时间增加了。Scrum鼓励团队成员只做几件事，从而给予每个项目应有的关注。通过将应用分解为更小的sprint，并将工作流程限制在这些sprint中，Scrum有助于更紧密地关注每个项目。

勇气——因为一个小的、跨职能的团队可以汇集他们的技能，而且因为Scrum鼓励密切合作，所以团队比个人更有勇气接受更大的挑战。

开放性——这是Scrum框架最重要的价值之一。Scrum团队可以通过定期与利益相关者进行审查，定期与同行进行计划和沟通来实现开放性。

承诺——Scrum团队承诺始终尽可能交付最高质量的工作，始终对涉众公开每个项目的真实状态。此外，Scrum团队承诺在每个Sprint中完成特定数量的工作。这一承诺与利益相关者建立了信任。

尊重——在Scrum团队中，尊重对于开放沟通非常重要，这样每个团队成员都可以放心地分享他们的障碍，团队可以互相帮助解决这些障碍。

当你参与一个真正的项目时，你会积累人们的知识，有助于建立团队精神，交付成功的项目，使客户成功，但不要忘记享受你所做的工作的乐趣!

Team Size

In contrast to the Waterfall way of work, where large groups of people work on long-term projects, the Agile methodology suggests forming a small cross-functional team to build an app rapidly. Don’t be surprised that a typical size of a team capable of developing a small app comprises three people. Naturally, you need a Product Owner who will be translating business needs into the project backlog items and prioritizing them. The development can be picked by the other two developers (Business Engineers). At the same time, the duties of the Scrum Master will be assigned to the lead developer.

Having such a small team keeps communication lines short and makes collaboration effective. This does not mean that once teams with larger team sizes are not effective! Such teams would require more coordination and clear communication agreements. Depending on the technical challenges the team is facing in their project, it might be necessary to involve Subject Matter Experts (SMEs). Note that SMEs are not accountable for product delivery. It’s the responsibility of the core Scrum team!

Another situation where the team size may get larger is when a company aims to start developing multiple apps. This requires more development power. Thus new rapid developers can join an already existing team to co-develop and gain more experience. But once they’ve skilled up, most likely, they will run a project in a smaller team.

团队规模

与瀑布式的工作方式相反，在瀑布式的工作方式中，一大群人在长期项目上工作，敏捷方法建议组建一个小的跨职能团队来快速构建一个应用程序。不要惊讶于一个能够开发小型应用的团队通常由3人组成。当然，您需要一个产品负责人，他将把业务需求转化为项目待办事项项，并对它们进行优先级排序。开发可以由其他两个开发人员(业务工程师)挑选。与此同时，Scrum Master的职责将分配给首席开发人员。

拥有这样一个小团队可以缩短沟通线，使协作更有效。这并不意味着团队规模越大，效率就越低!这些小组将需要更多的协调和明确的沟通协议。根据团队在其项目中面临的技术挑战，可能需要涉及主题专家(sme)。请注意，中小企业不负责产品交付。这是Scrum核心团队的责任!

另一种情况是，当一家公司打算开始开发多款应用时，团队规模可能会变得更大。这需要更多的开发力量。因此，新的快速开发人员可以加入一个已经存在的团队，共同开发和获得更多的经验。但一旦他们熟练起来，他们很可能会在一个较小的团队中运行一个项目。

Sprint 0

Before a Scrum team can start developing, they should familiarize themselves with the project. The most important things that should happen are getting an overall picture of the project, the organization, and the people involved. Important events that should happen are the official kick-off meeting and an initial Product Backlog Refinement meeting, conducted to ensure that the user stories taken into the first sprint are in a ‘ready’ state, meaning that they can be executed by the members of the Scrum team.

The period of one or two weeks preceding the actual development is so-called sprint 0. It is not an official Scrum event but is required to make the team ready for upcoming development. At this starting point, the whole team gets to know each other better. Product Owner and other stakeholders share information about the organization and the app requirements. It’s important to understand the overall picture so you have enough context to understand exactly what needs to be created.

Once the team composition is clear, the Scrum Master ensures that technical challenges requiring additional skills are identified. Think of UX/UI expert, integrations, or knowledge of IoT, Big Data, etc. To address these challenges, the Scrum Master books the availability of corresponding SMEs. As SME’s support multiple projects, it’s important to preserve their availability for the right sprint. Usually, you look several sprints ahead and make a forecast of required capacity and skills. To make this possible, Scrum Master creates a holiday/availability planning. Also, they create a list of contact details of your contacts and preferably reserve their availability to ask them questions if required.

During sprint 0, an official kick-off meeting takes place. During this meeting, all involved parties formalize the agreements, make clear who is responsible for what, discuss dependencies and confirm the set-up for the project. Creating transparency and clear collaboration agreements is the prerequisite for the project’s success.

Finally, make sure to conduct the Product Backlog Refinement meeting to make it clear which user stories will be picked up during the first sprint. You will learn more about these expectations for this meeting later on in this module.

Sprint 0

在Scrum团队开始开发之前，他们应该熟悉项目。应该发生的最重要的事情是对项目、组织和所涉及的人员有一个全面的了解。应该发生的重要事件是正式的启动会议和最初的产品待办事项细化会议，以确保进入第一个sprint的用户故事处于“就绪”状态，这意味着它们可以由Scrum团队成员执行。

在实际开发之前的一到两周时间被称为sprint 0。这不是一个正式的Scrum活动，但它是让团队为即将到来的开发做好准备所必需的。在这个起点上，整个团队可以更好地了解彼此。产品负责人和其他利益相关者共享有关组织和应用程序需求的信息。理解整体情况很重要，这样你就有足够的背景来准确理解需要创建什么。

一旦团队组成清晰，Scrum Master就会确保需要额外技能的技术挑战被识别出来。想想UX/UI专家，集成或物联网，大数据等知识。为了应对这些挑战，Scrum管理员记录了相应的中小企业的可用性。由于SME支持多个项目，因此为正确的sprint保留它们的可用性非常重要。通常，你会提前几个冲刺阶段，并预测所需的能力和技能。为了实现这一点，Scrum Master创建了一个假期/可用性计划。此外，他们还会列出你的联系人的详细联系方式，如果需要的话，最好保留他们的时间来问他们问题。

在sprint 0期间，将举行正式的启动会议。在会议期间，所有相关方将协议正式化，明确谁负责什么，讨论依赖关系并确认项目的设置。创建透明和明确的合作协议是项目成功的先决条件。

最后，确保召开产品待办事项细化会议，以明确在第一个冲刺期间将选择哪些用户故事。在本模块后面的部分中，您将了解更多关于这次会议的期望。

Scrum Events

The Agile process uses several events as prescribed by the Scrum guide. Each of these events has a dedicated goal, and they all happen in the prescribed order. The main purpose of these events is to have a coordinated way of clarifying business requirements, planning their implementation, monitoring the current progress, gathering required feedback, and making required improvements.

The four events that Scrum uses are:

  • Sprint Planning
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retrospective
  • Daily Stand-Up

Next to these events, the Scrum team performs Product Backlog Refinement. This is not an official Scrum event, but just like other listed events, this refinement happens regularly before all the other planned Scrum events. This is because, before you start working on any user stories, you need to know what to work on - hence the Product Backlog Refinement!

Each of the mentioned events has a dedicated goal, consumes a number of inputs, and delivers several outputs. Planning the schedule of these events in advance is helpful to ensure that all stakeholders can allocate time in their schedules.

Product Backlog Refinement

The meeting cadence starts with the Product Backlog Refinement meeting. The goal of this meeting is to reach a mutual understanding of user stories selected from the Product Backlog. This alignment is required because the Product Backlog can be amended by various people. What you want to prevent is that various parties incorrectly interpret these stories. During this meeting the backlog can be refined. PO will re-prioritize the backlog if needed. While assigning priorities to backlog items, PO takes into account the needs and wishes of stakeholders. But most importantly, PO focuses on the business value the user story will be leveraging. It’s important to prioritize the items from top to bottom, ensuring that top items are addressed first. Also it’s important to ensure that the team has refined the number of stories sufficient to fill up not one but two upcoming Sprints - this way, if priorities have changed along the way, there are “emergency” items the team can work on.

The product backlog refinement meetings require developers, PO, and occasionally SMEs to be present. Developers challenge PO to clarify the technical requirements to come up with a clear overview of user stories and corresponding tasks.

Remember, during the RAD training, you‘ve learned to estimate the complexity of user stories by assigning. The story points are based on the Fibonacci-like sequence (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 34 and, 55) and represent the effort it takes to implement the user story.

As the whole team needs to come to an agreement in regard to the story points assigned to a user story, sometimes opinions regarding the user story complexity differ. A planning poker is a tool that can be used to support the estimation of weights. Team members with at most low and at most high scores are asked to express their arguments explaining why a given user story is less/more challenging. The most important is to come to an agreement expressing the realistic user story complexity.

Although the number of story points assigned is determined by the details of the user story, the definition of done, and acceptance criteria, these weights may evolve over time. As the team becomes more experienced, the team velocity will increase, and certain challenges will become less difficult in the longer term.

At the end of this meeting, the team should have refined enough user stories for the upcoming sprint. The output of this meeting serves as the input for the Sprint Planning meeting.

产品待办事项列表细化

会议的节奏从产品待办事项列表细化会议开始。这次会议的目标是达成对从产品待办事项列表中选择的用户故事的相互理解。这种一致性是必需的，因为产品待办事项列表可以由不同的人修改。你要防止的是各方错误地解读这些故事。在这个会议期间，待办事项可以被细化。如果需要，PO将重新确定积压的优先级。在为待办事项分配优先级时，PO要考虑涉众的需求和愿望。但最重要的是，PO关注的是用户故事将利用的业务价值。从上到下对项目进行优先排序是很重要的，确保优先处理最重要的项目。同样重要的是要确保团队已经细化了故事的数量，足以填满不是一个而是两个即将到来的sprint——这样，如果优先级在此过程中发生了变化，团队就可以处理“紧急”项目。

产品待办事项列表细化会议需要开发人员、PO，偶尔还有中小企业出席。开发人员要求PO澄清技术需求，以便对用户描述和相应任务有一个清晰的概述。

记住，在RAD培训期间，您已经学会了通过分配来估计用户故事的复杂性。故事点基于类似斐波那契的序列(1、2、3、5、8、13、21、34和55)，并表示实现用户故事所需的努力。

当整个团队需要就分配给用户描述的描述点达成一致时，有时关于用户描述复杂性的意见会有所不同。计划扑克是一种工具，可用于支持权重估计。得分最高和最低的团队成员被要求表达他们的观点，解释为什么给定的用户故事更少/更具挑战性。最重要的是达成一致，表达现实的用户故事复杂性。

尽管分配的描述点的数量是由用户描述的细节、完成的定义和接受标准决定的，但是这些权重可能会随着时间的推移而变化。随着团队变得越来越有经验，团队的速度将会增加，从长远来看，某些挑战将变得不那么困难。

在会议结束时，团队应该为即将到来的sprint细化足够的用户故事。本次会议的输出将作为Sprint计划会议的输入。

Sprint planning

Sprint Planning is a collaborative process involving the Scrum Master, the Product Owner, and the entire Development Team. The Scrum Master is there to facilitate the meeting. Scrum Master also pays attention if SMEs need to be engaged. The Product Owner is responsible for clarifying the details of the Product Backlog items and their respective acceptance criteria. PO ensures that questions that remained unanswered during the Product Backlog Refinement meeting are addressed and all other unclarities resolved as much as possible. The Development Team defines the work and effort necessary to meet their Sprint commitment.

Product Owner ensures that the Dev Team has a clear goal and focus during the Sprint. During the Sprint Planning, the Development Team decides which user stories will be selected for the given Sprint. By defining the scope, the Development Team commits to accomplishing the selected user stories with high quality by the end of the Sprint. The team velocity is the factor that determines how much a team is capable of accomplishing during one Sprint. Knowing the team velocity, the team can almost precisely define how many user stories they can cover in the Sprint. Simply by aggregating the story points of all selected user stories, the team can check the feasibility of the selected workload. Changing the team composition can also impact the team velocity. Try to keep the team composition intact to reach an aligned and effective collaboration and speed up the product development.

Sprint计划

Sprint计划是一个涉及Scrum Master、产品负责人和整个开发团队的协作过程。Scrum Master的作用是促进会议的进行。Scrum Master也会关注中小企业是否需要参与进来。产品负责人负责澄清产品待办事项项的细节及其各自的接受标准。PO确保在产品待办事项细化会议期间未解决的问题得到解决，并尽可能解决所有其他不明确的问题。开发团队定义完成Sprint承诺所需的工作和努力。

产品负责人确保开发团队在Sprint期间有一个明确的目标和重点。在Sprint计划期间，开发团队决定为给定的Sprint选择哪些用户情景。通过定义范围，开发团队承诺在Sprint结束时以高质量完成选定的用户描述。团队速度是决定团队在一个Sprint期间能够完成多少工作的因素。了解了团队的速度，团队几乎可以精确地定义他们在Sprint中可以覆盖多少用户故事。仅仅通过聚合所有选定用户描述的描述点，团队就可以检查所选工作负载的可行性。改变团队组成也会影响团队速度。尽量保持团队组成完整，以达到一致和有效的协作，加快产品开发。

Sprint review

By the end of the Sprint, Scrum Master organizes a Sprint Review. This event is aimed to demonstrate the result of the Sprint to the Product Owner, Management, and end-users. When reviewing the product, the starting point is the Sprint goal, where the scope has been set to address a set of user stories.

During the demo, acceptance criteria are used to check whether the presented increment satisfies the Definition of Done. Feedback received from the PO and another stakeholder may result in a new user story added in the next Sprint, or the Product Backlog may need to undergo some adjustments.

The result of the sprint review is a set of potential new backlog items that are approved by the PO.

Sprint审查

在Sprint结束时，Scrum Master会组织一次Sprint Review。此事件旨在向产品负责人、管理层和最终用户演示Sprint的结果。当审查产品时，起点是Sprint目标，在这个目标中，范围被设置为处理一组用户场景。

在演示过程中，验收标准用于检查呈现的增量是否满足完成的定义。从PO和其他涉众那里收到的反馈可能会导致在下一个Sprint中添加新的用户描述，或者产品待办事项列表可能需要进行一些调整。

冲刺评审的结果是PO批准的一组潜在的新待办事项。

Sprint retrospective

Retrospective meetings are typically held at the end of each Sprint or at the end of a major development cycle, and are critical to opening communication channels. After the product increment has been reviewed by the Product Owner, it’s time for the Scrum team to hold a Retrospective. During this meeting, facilitated by the Scrum Master, everyone gives feedback about what went well, what needs to be improved, or which new interventions need to be undertaken. Such a meeting may take up to 1,5 hours. It’s a good opportunity to reflect on the process, people, and organizational aspects of the completed Sprint.

Scrum Master needs to create an environment where every team member feels comfortable to share their feedback. Every member should be able to share their honest opinion with respect to the rest of the team.

There are several things that can be evaluated during this meeting:

  • What did go well, and what should you keep doing?
  • What did not go well, and how can it be improved?
  • What are the new things that a team member may start doing, and why?

The most common setup is that each team member lists five items per category. Then by voting the team can decide which three improvement points will be included in the process of the new Sprint. One of the common pitfalls here is an attempt to change too much at once, which won’t lead to the desired process.

Every improvement point is the responsibility of the whole team but must have an owner to keep an eye on this specific point.

Sprint回顾

回顾会议通常在每个Sprint结束或主要开发周期结束时举行，对于打开沟通渠道至关重要。在产品负责人审查了产品增量之后，Scrum团队就该举行一次回顾会议了。在会议期间，在Scrum Master的推动下，每个人都给出了关于哪些进展顺利，哪些需要改进，或者需要采取哪些新的干预措施的反馈。这样的会议可能需要长达1.5个小时。这是一个很好的机会来反思完成Sprint的过程、人员和组织方面。

Scrum Master需要创造一个环境，让每个团队成员都能舒服地分享他们的反馈。每个成员都应该能够与团队的其他成员分享他们诚实的意见。

在这次会议中，有几件事可以评估:

-什么进展顺利，你应该继续做什么?

-哪里不顺利，如何改进?

-团队成员可能开始做的新事情是什么，为什么?

最常见的设置是每个团队成员在每个类别中列出五个项目。然后，通过投票，团队可以决定哪三个改进点将包含在新的Sprint过程中。这里常见的陷阱之一是试图一次改变太多，这不会导致期望的过程。

每个改进点都是整个团队的责任，但必须有一个所有者来关注这个特定的点。

Daily scrum

In the daily scrum, the Scrum team members we share on a daily basis the progress with the team. This is typically attended by the developers, but the PO is also welcomed. Every developer shares the following three things:

  • What have I done since the last daily scrum?
  • What am I going to do until the next daily scrum?
  • What are things holding me back, my impediments?

It has been proven that holding a meeting where everyone has to physically stand up, shows to be quick and effective. To ensure that stand-up meetings do not exceed the dedicated 15 minutes, it’s important to address some rules for holding such a meeting. The goal of this meeting is not to update the Scrum Master, but to inform the team members. This meeting is not aimed to elaborate on solutions or to solve issues but to inform others if you need their input or help. In addition, the team will usually look at the list of items planned versus items done (usually called a burndown chart), which measures the amount of work completed versus the total required by the end of the Sprint.

每日例会

在每日scrum中，我们的scrum团队成员每天与团队分享进度。这通常由开发人员参加，但PO也很受欢迎。每个开发者都会分享以下三点:

自上次每日例会以来，我做了什么?

在下一次每日例会之前我该做些什么?

是什么阻碍了我，阻碍了我?

事实证明，召开一个会议，每个人都必须站起来，显示出快速和有效。为了确保站立会议不超过规定的15分钟，重要的是要遵守召开此类会议的一些规则。这次会议的目的不是更新Scrum Master，而是通知团队成员。这个会议的目的不是详细说明解决方案或解决问题，而是告诉别人你是否需要他们的意见或帮助。此外，团队通常会查看计划的项目与已完成的项目的列表(通常称为燃尽图)，它测量完成的工作量与Sprint结束时所需的总工作量。

Location

For Mendix apps, we recommend working in a single place, so you have short communication lines with a customer. Physical co-location allows to set up a Scrum room with a scrum white-board so that anyone interested can view the current status of the team. In conditions of virtual collaboration, it is the responsibility of the Scrum Master to create and update a digital Scrum board, make it accessible to all relevant stakeholders, and organize regular virtual meetings to facilitate the Scrum events.

An offshoring model is based on collaboration across multiple time zones. Offshoring makes some things more complicated due to different native languages and time differences. When planning the availability of SMEs located in different time zones, the Scrum Master should keep an eye on potential delays.

Multiple Associations Between Entities

Before you dive into new theory, let’s take a look at a quick recap of what you’ve learned in the Rapid Developer learning path.

In Mendix there are three types of associations. These associations allow you to create possible connections between objects. These connections are also called references. The Mendix platform has the following association types:

  • One-to-Many (1 - *)
  • One-to-One (1 – 1)
  • Many-to-Many (* - *)

In the Rapid Developer learning path, you used single associations between entities. There was always only one association between two entities. For example: a single One-to-Many association between Teacher and Training Event.

It is however also possible to add multiple associations between two entities. The most important reason to do this, is because you can have different relations between instances of entities. A company could for example wish to store two addresses for customers: one shipping address and one invoice address.

The company wants to be able to see a data grid in their app with all customers, and in that data grid show both addresses immediately. This is impossible when you use a One-to-Many association from Customer to Address, using an enumeration to see what type of address it is. With multiple One-to-One associations, it is possible to directly output both addresses in the data grid.

Using a One-to-Many association versus multiple One-to-One associations also impacts the way the end user can set certain connections.

In the end, what you choose, mostly comes down to what the end user wants in the app front-end wise. How does he want to see and manipulate data?

实体之间的多重关联

在您深入研究新的理论之前，让我们快速回顾一下您在快速开发人员学习路径中学到的内容。

在Mendix中有三种类型的关联。这些关联允许您在对象之间创建可能的连接。这些连接也称为引用。Mendix平台有以下关联类型:

一对多(1 - *)

一对一(1 - 1)

多对多(* - *)

在快速开发人员学习路径中，您使用实体之间的单个关联。两个实体之间总是只有一个关联。例如:教师和培训事件之间的单个一对多关联。

但是，也可以在两个实体之间添加多个关联。这样做的最重要的原因是，实体的实例之间可以有不同的关系。例如，公司可能希望为客户存储两个地址:一个送货地址和一个发票地址。

该公司希望能够在他们的应用程序中看到所有客户的数据网格，并在该数据网格中立即显示两个地址。当您使用从Customer到Address的一对多关联，使用枚举查看它是什么类型的地址时，这是不可能的。使用多个一对一关联，可以在数据网格中直接输出两个地址。

使用一对多关联和多个一对一关联还会影响最终用户设置某些连接的方式。

最后，你的选择主要取决于最终用户对应用前端的需求。他希望如何查看和操作数据?

Players and Captains

Now you know about the possibility to add multiple associations between two entities, it’s time to investigate how this can help Adrian and the Soccer Squad app.
Imagine this: a soccer team can have multiple players (in fact, they need at least 11). This is the perfect example of a One-to-Many association. However, each team can only have one captain, who is also a player.
You could solve this by adding a Boolean attribute to the Player entity; Captain. This will result in an input widget every time you create or edit a player, is this the captain yes or no?

球员和队长

现在你知道了在两个实体之间添加多个关联的可能性，是时候研究这如何帮助Adrian和足球队应用程序了。

想象一下:一支足球队可以有多名球员(实际上，他们至少需要11名球员)。这是一对多关联的完美示例。然而，每队只能有一名队长，他也是一名球员。

你可以通过向Player实体添加布尔属性来解决这个问题;队长。这将导致每次创建或编辑球员时都会出现一个输入小部件，这是队长是还是不是?

Perhaps you think this solution is just fine, and that’s ok! But let’s take a look at a better solution, using two associations between the Team and Player entities.

也许你认为这个解决方案很好，那没关系!但是让我们看看一个更好的解决方案，使用Team和Player实体之间的两个关联。

With this Domain Model structure, you can add many players to the team and select one of those players as the Captain. The second association provides a second benefit: because it is a One-to-One association, you can immediately only have one Captain. This saves you the work of having to create custom logic to enforce this!
Another example in the Soccer Squad app where you can apply multiple associations between two entities, is the association from Match to Team. A match is played by two teams, the home team and the away team. There is no One-to-Two association in Mendix. And, if you simply use a Many-to-Many association, it’s impossible to store who the home team is.

使用这个Domain Model结构，您可以向团队中添加许多玩家，并选择其中一个玩家作为队长。第二个关联提供了第二个好处:因为它是一对一的关联，所以您可以立即只拥有一个Captain。这节省了您创建自定义逻辑来执行此操作的工作!

另一个例子是在Soccer Squad应用程序中，你可以在两个实体之间应用多个关联，从Match到Team的关联。一场比赛由两支球队进行，主队和客队。Mendix中不存在一对二关联。而且，如果您只是使用多对多关联，则不可能存储主队是谁。

You could solve this by using the Information Entity pattern, as discussed in the Rapid Developer learning path. But, this solution adds three database tables to your database and you would need to create custom logic to enforce that only two teams can be added to a match. Also, what would this entity be called? MatchRole? MatchTeamType? This solution is already unclear before you can even get started, and that is never a good thing in application development!
Instead, you can use two associations between the Match and Team entities, one for Match_TeamHome and one for Match_TeamAway. This automatically enforces that you can only select two teams per match. It only adds two simple database tables to your database and the naming is super clear! It seems we have a winner (pun intended)!

您可以通过使用信息实体模式来解决这个问题，正如快速开发人员学习路径中所讨论的那样。但是，此解决方案将三个数据库表添加到数据库中，并且您需要创建自定义逻辑来强制只能将两个团队添加到比赛中。另外，这个实体将被称为什么?MatchRole吗?MatchTeamType吗?在您开始之前，这个解决方案就已经不清楚了，这在应用程序开发中从来都不是一件好事!

相反，您可以在Match和Team实体之间使用两个关联，一个用于Match_TeamHome，另一个用于Match_TeamAway。这自动强制您每场比赛只能选择两支球队。它只向数据库中添加两个简单的数据库表，并且命名非常清晰!看来我们有赢家了(双关语)!
